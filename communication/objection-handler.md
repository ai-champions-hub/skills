# Objection Handler

> Draft responses to tough objections — sales calls, negotiations, stakeholder pushback.

## Context

You're preparing for a conversation where you expect pushback — a sales call, a budget negotiation, a stakeholder review, or an internal pitch. You know the objections that are likely to come up and you want polished, confident responses ready in advance.

## Instructions

1. I will give you:
   - The context (what I'm pitching/proposing and to whom).
   - A list of objections I expect to hear.
2. For each objection, generate a response using this framework:
   - **Acknowledge**: show you heard the concern (1 sentence, no dismissing).
   - **Reframe**: shift the perspective or correct a misconception (1-2 sentences).
   - **Evidence**: provide a specific data point, example, or analogy (1-2 sentences).
   - **Bridge**: transition back to the value of your proposal (1 sentence).
3. Keep each response under 80 words — these need to feel conversational, not scripted.
4. Write in first person, as if I'm speaking out loud.
5. Match the formality level to the audience (e.g., casual for internal team, professional for enterprise client).
6. After all individual responses, add a section called "If all else fails" with one universal fallback response that works for any unexpected objection.

## Example

### Input

```
Context: I'm pitching our analytics platform to a VP of Marketing at a mid-size SaaS company. 
Objections:
1. "We already use Google Analytics, why would we switch?"
2. "Your pricing is too high for our budget."
3. "We don't have the bandwidth to onboard a new tool right now."
```

### Output

**1. "We already use Google Analytics, why would we switch?"**

Totally fair — GA is a solid starting point and I wouldn't suggest ripping it out. The difference is that GA tells you *what* happened on your site, while our platform connects that to *why* it happened and *what to do next*. One of our customers, a SaaS company your size, found that combining intent signals with traffic data increased their conversion rate by 22% in the first quarter. It's less of a switch and more of an upgrade layer.

**2. "Your pricing is too high for our budget."**

I appreciate you being upfront about that. The way I'd think about it is cost per insight rather than sticker price — if one campaign optimization pays for the annual license, everything after that is upside. Our mid-market customers typically see ROI within 60 days. Happy to walk through a quick model with your actual numbers to see if it pencils out.

**3. "We don't have the bandwidth to onboard a new tool right now."**

That's the last thing I'd want — to add to your team's plate. Our onboarding is designed for busy teams: median time to first dashboard is 3 days, and we assign a dedicated CSM who does the heavy lifting. Most teams are fully up and running in under two weeks without pulling anyone off their current work.

**If all else fails**

"That's a really fair concern and I don't want to brush past it. Can I take that back to my team, put together a concrete answer, and follow up by Thursday? I want to make sure we address it properly rather than give you a hand-wavy response right now."
